Fishes

Introduction

Fish are animals that live in water and breathe through gills. They are covered in scales, have fins to swim, and are cold-blooded. Fish come in all shapes, sizes, and colors—from tiny goldfish to huge sharks! There are over 30,000 different kinds of fish in the world.

Where Do Fishes Live?

Fish live in water, and they can be found in many different places!


Fish like clownfish, tuna, and sharks live in salty seawater.

Fish like trout, catfish, and piranhas live in freshwater.

Some fish, like the anglerfish, live in the dark, deep parts of the ocean.

These underwater "cities" are home to colorful fish like parrotfish and butterflyfish.

Interesting Features

Here are some things that make fishes special:

GILLS FOR BREATHING

Fish don’t have lungs like us. They use gills to take oxygen from the water.

FINS FOR SWIMMING

Fish use fins to move through water and steer in different directions.

SCALES AND SLIME

Fish are covered in scales that protect their bodies. They also have a slippery layer of slime that helps them glide through water.

LAY EGGS

Most fish lay eggs, but some, like guppies, give birth to live young.

COLD-BLOODED

Fish can’t control their body temperature, so it changes with the water around them.

GREAT SENSES

Fish have excellent senses of sight, smell, and even hearing. Some fish can feel tiny movements in the water using special lines on their bodies called lateral lines.

Fun Fact!!

The whale shark is the biggest fish in the world—it can grow as long as a school bus! On the other hand, the dwarf goby is one of the smallest fish, only as big as a fingernail.
