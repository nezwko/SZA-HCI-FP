FISHES

What Are Fish?

Fish are fascinating creatures that live in water and come in all shapes, sizes, and colors. They are one of the oldest and most diverse groups of animals on Earth!

1. Fish Have Scales

Fish have smooth, protective scales that cover their bodies. These scales help them swim easily and stay safe from harm in the water.

2. Fish Breathe Through Gills

Unlike mammals, fish use gills to breathe underwater. Gills take oxygen from the water, allowing fish to live in oceans, rivers, and lakes.

3. Fish Are Cold-Blooded

Fish are cold-blooded, meaning their body temperature matches the temperature of the water around them. When the water is cold, they slow down; when it's warm, they become more active.

4. Fish Lay Eggs

Most fish lay eggs in the water. Baby fish, called fry, hatch from these eggs and grow in the water. Some fish, like sharks, give birth to live babies.

5. Fish Have Fins

Fish have fins that help them swim smoothly through the water. They use their fins to steer, stop, and balance. Some fish also have tails that help them swim fast!

6. Fish Live in Water

Fish are aquatic animals, meaning they live in water. They can be found in oceans, rivers, lakes, and even ponds. Some fish live deep in the ocean, while others live closer to the surface.
