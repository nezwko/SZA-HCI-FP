What Are Reptiles?

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals that live on land and in water. They have some cool features that make them unique and fascinating!

1. Reptiles Have Scales

Instead of fur or feathers, reptiles have dry, scaly skin that helps protect them. The scales can be different shapes and sizes, depending on the reptile.

2. Reptiles Are Cold-Blooded

Reptiles can't make their own body heat like mammals. Instead, they rely on the sun to warm up and keep them active.

3. Reptiles Lay Eggs

Most reptiles lay eggs, though some give birth to live babies. Their eggs have hard shells to protect the babies inside.

Many reptiles have sharp teeth and claws for hunting or protecting themselves. Some, like crocodiles, have very strong jaws!

6. Reptiles Live in Many Habitats

Reptiles can live in deserts, forests, oceans, and even jungles. They are found on every continent except Antarctica.
