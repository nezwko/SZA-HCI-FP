Mammals

Introduction

Mammals are a special group of animals that are warm-blooded and have hair or fur on their bodies. They are different from other animals because mammal moms feed their babies milk. You are a mammal too! Mammals are found all over the world and come in many shapes and sizes, from tiny bats to giant elephants.

Where Do Mammals Live?

Mammals can live almost anywhere on Earth!


LAND

Many mammals, like lions, tigers, and rabbits, live on land in forests, grasslands, or deserts.

WATER

Some mammals, like dolphins, whales, and seals, live in the ocean.

SKY

Bats are the only mammals that can truly fly! They live in caves, trees, or even under bridges.

COLD PLACES

Mammals adapt to their habitats by growing fur for cold places, like polar bears in the Arctic, or having special bodies to swim, like otters and dolphins.

Interesting Features

Here are some things that make mammals special:

HAIR OR FUR

Mammals have hair or fur that helps keep them warm. Some have a lot, like bears, while others have a little, like dolphins.

LIVE BABIES

Most mammals give birth to live babies (not eggs), and the mothers take care of them.

MILK FOR BABIES

Mother mammals produce milk to feed their babies. This is super important for baby mammals to grow.

BIG BRAINS

Mammals are smart! They can solve problems, play, and even learn new things

LUNGS FOR BREATHING

All mammals breathe air through their lungs, even those that live underwater.

Fun Fact!!

The blue whale is the largest mammal in the world—it’s as big as three school buses! On the other hand, the bumblebee bat is the smallest mammal, and it’s about the size of a penny.
