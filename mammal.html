What Are Mammals?

Mammals are one of the most fascinating groups of animals on Earth! From the tiniest mouse to the largest whale, mammals come in all shapes and sizes and can be found all over the world. But what exactly makes an animal a mammal? Let’s dive in and find out!

1. Mammals Have Hair or Fur

All mammals have hair or fur, even if it’s just a little. Some, like lions, are super furry, while others, like dolphins, have just a tiny bit!

2. Mammals Breathe Air

Mammals need air to live, just like you! They use their lungs to breathe, whether they live on land or in water.

3. Mammals Drink Milk as Babies

When mammals are babies, they drink milk from their mothers. This gives them the nutrients they need to grow healthy and strong!

4. Mammals Are Warm-Blooded

Mammals can keep their bodies warm no matter the weather. This helps them survive in all kinds of places, from snowy mountains to sunny deserts.

5. Mammals Are Everywhere!

Mammals live all over the world, from the deepest oceans to the highest mountains.

6. Mammals Come in All Shapes and Sizes

Mammals can be as big as a blue whale (the biggest animal in the world!) or as small as a bumblebee bat.
