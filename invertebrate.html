Invertebrates

Introduction

Invertebrates are animals that don’t have backbones. Instead of bones, they might have soft bodies, shells, or tough outer coverings. Invertebrates are everywhere, and they make up about 95% of all animals on Earth! Some common invertebrates are insects, spiders, jellyfish, snails, and octopuses.

Where Do Invertebrates Live?

Invertebrates live in almost every part of the world!


OCEAN

Many invertebrates, like jellyfish, starfish, and crabs, live in the sea.

LAND

Insects, worms, and snails can be found in forests, gardens, and deserts.

AIR

Insects like bees and butterflies can fly through the air.

UNDERGROUND

Ants and other small invertebrates live in the soil, helping plants grow by loosening the dirt.

Interesting Features

Here are some things that make invertebrates special:

NO BACKBONES

Unlike us, invertebrates don’t have backbones. Some have hard shells (like snails), while others have soft, squishy bodies (like jellyfish).

SMALL SIZE

Most invertebrates are tiny, but some, like the giant squid, can grow very large.

EXOSKELETONS

Many invertebrates, like ants and crabs, have a hard shell on the outside of their bodies to protect them.

LAY EGGS

Invertebrates often lay lots of tiny eggs to make sure their babies survive.

WAYS TO MOVE

Some crawl (like worms), some fly (like butterflies), and some swim (like squid).

HELPFUL AND HARMFUL

Some invertebrates, like bees, help us by pollinating plants. Others, like mosquitoes, can be pests.

Fun Fact!!

The largest invertebrate is the giant squid, which can be as long as a school bus! The smallest is a tiny insect called a fairyfly, which is smaller than a grain of salt.
