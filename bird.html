Birds

Introduction

Birds are animals with feathers, wings, and beaks. They are best known for flying, though not all birds can fly. Birds are unique because they lay eggs, and they are found all over the world. Some are tiny like hummingbirds, while others, like ostriches, are huge and can’t fly.

Where Do Birds Live?

Birds can live in many different places, depending on their type:


FOREST

Many birds, like woodpeckers and owls, live in trees.

OCEAN

Seabirds, like seagulls and penguins, live near water.

GRASSLAND OR DESERT

Birds like eagles and ostriches live in open areas like grasslands or deserts.

CITY

Birds like pigeons and sparrows often make their homes near people. Birds build nests to lay their eggs, and these can be in trees, cliffs, or even on the ground.

Interesting Features

Here are some things that make birds special:

FEATHERS

Birds are the only animals with feathers. Feathers help them fly, keep them warm, and even attract mates.

WINGS

Most birds have wings and can fly, though some, like penguins and ostriches, use their wings for other purposes like swimming or balance.

BEAKS

Birds don’t have teeth; instead, they use their beaks to eat, build nests, or fight. Different birds have different beak shapes depending on what they eat.

LAY EGGS

Birds lay hard-shelled eggs, and the parents usually keep them warm until the chicks hatch.

LIGHT BONES

Birds have light, hollow bones filled with air spaces, which not only make their bodies lighter but also provide strength for the muscles they use to fly.

GREAT VISION

Birds often have excellent eyesight to spot food or predators from far away.

Fun Fact!!

The smallest bird in the world is the bee hummingbird, which is as tiny as a thumb, and the largest is the ostrich, which can grow taller than a grown-up person!
